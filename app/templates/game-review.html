Crusaders' ride suffocates Broughton in 4A East opener

Cardinal Gibbons put on a masterclass in transition offense and defensive pressure, cruising to a 14–6 win that wasn't as close as the final score suggests.

From the opening whistle, Cardinal Gibbons made their intentions clear. The Crusaders won the first faceoff, pushed in transition, and buried one within the first 45 seconds. It was a sign of things to come for a Broughton squad that spent most of the night chasing.

The story of this game was Gibbons' ride. Every time Broughton tried to clear, they ran into a wall of pressure at midfield. The Crusaders forced 8 turnovers off their ride alone in the first half, converting five of those into goals. Broughton's clearing percentage sat at a brutal 42% through three quarters before the Crusaders eased off in the fourth with the game well in hand.

First half: transition clinic

Gibbons scored four of their first-half goals in transition, and it was their middies who were making it happen. The wing play on faceoffs was crisp — winning the clamp and immediately pushing numbers the other way before Broughton's defense could get set.

The second quarter was the dagger. Five goals in the frame, including three in a two-minute stretch that turned a 5-2 lead into an 8-3 laugher. By halftime, Gibbons led 9-3 and the outcome was essentially decided.

"We talked all week about making them uncomfortable in their clearing game. The guys executed the ride exactly how we drew it up."

— Gibbons Head Coach, postgame

Second half: managing the clock

To their credit, Broughton came out with more energy in the third quarter and won three of the four faceoffs in the frame. But Gibbons' defense was stifling in the half-field, limiting the Capitals to just one goal on seven shots in the third.

The Crusaders milked clock in the fourth, content to swing the ball and burn time rather than push the pace. The two late Broughton goals came against reserves with the game already decided, which makes the 14-6 final look more competitive than the game actually felt.

What this means

Cardinal Gibbons is the real deal. They're 6-0 now and their schedule has included some legit tests — this was just the most dominant performance yet. The ride is elite, the faceoff game is a weapon, and they have depth at every position. They'll be the team to beat in 4A East.

For Broughton, it's back to the drawing board on the clear. They have athletes and they can score in the half-field, but you can't turn it over eight times in a half against a team this good and expect to hang around. The good news is it's early March and there's time to fix it.
